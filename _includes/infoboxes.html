Facts

Childhood drowning is the leading cause of injury-related death in children ages 0-5. In 2016, there were 155 incidents with 49 fatalities. 16 children and 33 adults/teens lost their lives in Arizona due to drowning. Tragically, these deaths could have been prevented with life jackets, proper water barriers, education, and CPR training.

Involvement

After the loss of their twenty-one year old son, Ryan Thomas, at Saguaro Lake in 2008, the Thomas family created the Ryan Thomas Foundation in hopes of making a difference in the lives of others. Directed and run entirely by volunteers, the Ryan Thomas Foundation aims to contribute all of it's event proceeds to local causes.

Accomplishments

In addition to donations to organizations that promote water safetey education, he Foundation has also worked with the Arizona Game and Fish department to assist in the installation and maintenance of 18 life-saving life jacket kiosks at several Arizona lakes.
